CV
Mathilde Krampitz is a senior research assistant at IKEM. She works on projects focused on autonomous and connected driving.
She studied law at University of Potsdam with a focus on theories of law. After completing the first state examination, she completed her legal clerkship in Berlin and Rio de Janeiro. She gained further professional experience at the law firms Heuking Kühn Lüer Wojtek and Freshfields Bruckhaus Deringer.
Mathilde Krampitz has special knowledge in the field of technology and innovation law. In addition to governmental issues concerning the approval of automated and connected driving, she focuses on questions regarding the formalization of legal norms, data protection law, approval and liability law, especially in the automotive sector.
