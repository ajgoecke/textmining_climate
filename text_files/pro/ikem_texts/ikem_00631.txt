CV
Heiner von Lüpke deals with questions of international climate policy since 2004, with a focus on issues of international cooperation, climate finance and sectoral integration of climate policy. In this context, he worked for the United Nations Food and Agriculture Organization (UN-FAO) for 3 years as a specialist in forest and climate change (2004-2007). This included advice and support for the UN member states on issues relating to UNFCCC reporting, UNFCCC negotiations related to forests and land use change, as well as capacity building for CDM and REDD +. From 2007 to 2015 Heiner worked for the German bilateral Cooperation agency GIZ as policy advisor to the Indonesian government. His main focus was on advising for the 13th Conference of the Parties to the UNFCCC in 2007 hosted by Indonesia, as well as the subsequent development of national climate policy frameworks. From 2015 to 2017 he advised the German Federal Government on issues of international climate policy and climate finance for GIZ. Topics of international climate finance, especially from a research perspective on interactions between donors and recipient countries and resulting effects on national policy developments has been the focus of his work for the German Institute for Economic Research (DIW Berlin) 2017-2020.
Heiner von Lüpke holds university degrees as a Forestry Engineer (FH Hildesheim-Holzminden, 2000), Master of Tropical Forestry and Management, (TU Dresden, 2003), and Latin American Studies (TU Dresden, 2003). His doctoral thesis at the Political Science Faculty of the TU Darmstadt (2016 – 2021) includes a comparative analysis of integration processes between climate and sectoral policy domains in Mexico and Indonesia.
