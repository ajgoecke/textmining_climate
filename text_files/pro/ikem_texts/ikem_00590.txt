Fanny Knoll is a senior research associate at the IKEM and a member of the chair for public law, financial law, environmental and energy law of Prof. Dr. Rodi at the University of Greifswald. She studied law at the Freie Universität Berlin. She is currently doing her doctorate on the subject of energy network stability under Prof. Dr. Rodi at the University of Greifswald.
After completing her First State Examination, she worked for the German Bundestag in the parliamentary groups (international area) for one year. She then completed her legal clerkship at the Kammergericht Berlin. Her legal clerkship stations included the law firm Taylor Wessing Partnerschaftsgesellschaft mbB, the Federal Ministry of Labour and Social Affairs (international section) and the Federal Ministry of Foreign Affairs / Permanent Mission to the United Nations in Geneva (Switzerland).
Fanny Knoll’s work focuses on energy and data law, with particular emphasis on the digitalisation of the energy system transformation in conjunction with Smart Grid, artificial intelligence, blockchain and IT security.
